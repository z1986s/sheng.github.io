  • Homework 8 - Due Friday, May 19, 2023
    • Read Sections 8.2, 10.3
    • Do the following problems:
      1. Page 201-202, #11, 13, 20, 22
      2. Let $X$ be a normed linear space. $C_1$ and $C_2$ are closed convex sets in $X$ such that $C_1+B=C_2+B$ for some bounded set $B$. Prove that $C_1=C_2$.
      3. Let $E$ be the subset of $C[0,1]$ consisting of nowhere differentiable functions. Prove that $E$ is a residual set in $C[0,1]$.
        (Hint: consider for every $n\in\mathbb{N}$ the set $$F_n:=\{f\in C[0,1]: \text{there exists}~x_0\in [0,1]~\text{such that}~|f(x)-f(x_0)|\le n|x-x_0|~\text{for all}~x\in [0,1]\}.$$ Show that (i) $F_n$ is closed in $C[0,1]$; (ii) the complement of $F_n$ is dense in $C[0,1]$, and hence $F_n$ has empty interior; (iii) the complement of $\bigcup_{n=1}^\infty F_n$, which is residual by (i) and (ii), is a subset of $E$.)
      4. Prove that every separable normed linear space embeds linearly isometrically into $\ell_\infty$.
      5. Let $X$ be a separable Banach space. Show that there is a bounded linear operator from $\ell_1$ onto $X$. (Hint: little open mapping theorem)
      6. Let $\{x_n\}_{n=1}^\infty$ be a sequence in $[-\pi,\pi]$. Prove that there exist a function $f\in C[-\pi,\pi]$ and a residual set $D$ in $[-\pi,\pi]$ such that $D\supseteq\{x_n\}_{n=1}^\infty$ and the Fourier series of $f$ diverges at every point in $D$.

  • Homework 6 - Due Friday, April 21, 2023
    • Read Section 10.1
    • Do the following problems:
      1. Page 161, #1, 2, 6, 7
      2. Let $f\in L_\infty$. Prove that $\lim_{p\to\infty}\|f\|_p=\|f\|_\infty$.
      3. Let $f\in L_p$ for some $p>1$. Prove that $\lim_{q\to1+}\|f\|_q=\|f\|_1$.

      7. Let $p\in[1, \infty]$. Prove that $\ell_p$ is linearly isometric to a subspace of $L_p$.

  • Homework 4 - Due Thursday, March 30, 2023
    • Read Section 8.1
    • Do the following problems:
      1. Page 147, problems 11, 12, 13, 14, 15
      2. Let $X$ and $Y$ be metric spaces and $Y$ is complete. $A$ is a dense subset of $X$, and $f: A\to Y$ is a uniformly continuous map. Prove that $f$ extends to a uniformly continuous map $\tilde{f}: X\to Y$. If moreover $f$ is Lipschitz, then so is $\tilde{f}$, and $\text{Lip}(f)=\text{Lip}(\tilde{f})$.
