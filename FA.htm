Homework

  • Homework 3 - Due Friday, March 24, 2023
    • Read Section 7.8
    • Do the following problems:
      1. Page 147, #4, 5, 6, 10
      2. Prove that there is no map $T: L_0\equiv L_0[0,1]\to\mathbb R$ that satisfies $T(f_n)\to 0\Longleftrightarrow f\to 0$ a.e.
      3. Prove that every separable metric space embeds isometrically into $\ell_\infty$. In particular, every $n$-point metric space embeds isometrically into $\ell^{n-1}_\infty$.
      4. Let $\delta>0$. Prove that every unbounded metric space $(M, d)$ has a $\delta$-skeleton, i.e., a subset $S$ that satisfies: (1) for every $x\in M$ there exists $s\in S$ such that $d(x, s)\le\delta$, and (2) $d(u,v)\ge\delta$ for every $u,v\in S$, $u\neq v$.

  • Homework 2 - Due Friday, March 17, 2023
    • Read Sections 7.3, 7.4, 7.6
    • Do the following problems:
      1. Page 146, #1, 2
      2. Let $V$ be a vector space and $\phi_1,...,\phi_n, \psi\in V^\prime$. Prove that $\psi\in\rm{span}\{\phi_1,...,\phi_n\}$ if and only if $\bigcap_{i=1}^n \ker\phi_i\subseteq\ker\psi$.
      3. Let $A$ be a symmetric convex set in a vector space $V$. Show that the Minkowski functional of $A$, defined by $p_A(x):=\inf\{\lambda>0: x\in\lambda A\}$, is a semi-norm on $\rm{span}(A)$.
      4. Let $A$ be a set in $\mathbb R^n$. Show that every point in $\rm{co}(A)$ can be written as a convex combination of at most $n+1$ points in $A$.
